Day 1 • Module 1

The Freight Triangle Ecosystem

To attract the best carriers, you must first understand the complete ecosystem. Successful dispatchers don't just "find loads." They manage the friction between the three pillars of the supply chain.

1. Carriers (Owner-Operators)

Goal: Maximum profitability and drive time.
Pain Points: Deadheading, waiting at docks unpaid, dealing with broker setups, invoicing, and rate fluctuations.

2. Brokers

Goal: Reliable capacity for their shippers.
Pain Points: Trucks falling off loads, lack of communication in transit, missed appointments, and carrier fraud.

3. Direct Shippers

Goal: Consistent, predictable supply chain.
Pain Points: Spot market volatility, damaged freight, inability to forecast logistics spend.

Day 1 • Module 2

Pain Point Marketing (The Hook)

Stop sounding like every other beginner dispatcher on Facebook. If your pitch is "I charge 8% and will book your loads," you are competing on price (a race to the bottom).

You must pivot to Pain Point Marketing. Address the specific headaches you learned in Module 1.

❌ The Amateur Pitch

"Need a dispatcher? We have the best rates. PM me. 8% fee."

✅ The Crown Access Pitch

"Tired of eating deadhead miles out of the Midwest? I specialize in keeping flatbeds moving at $3.00+/mile on continuous triangle routes, and I handle 100% of your broker packets and invoicing so you just drive."

Notice the difference? The second pitch identifies a target (Flatbeds in the Midwest), states a solution ($3.00+ on triangle routes), and relieves an administrative headache (packets/invoicing).

Day 1 • Module 3

Building Your Digital Storefront

When a carrier or broker hears your pitch, the very next thing they do is Google you. If you only have a Gmail address and no website, you instantly lose trust.

The Non-Negotiable Setup:

  • Professional Website: Must clearly list your services, target equipment types, and include a contact form. It proves you are a legitimate agency.
  • Custom Domain Email: You must use `name@youragency.com`, not `youragency@gmail.com`. Brokers are heavily vetting for fraud; custom emails are a basic requirement.
  • Google Business Profile: Register your business locally. This captures local owner-operators searching for "truck dispatching near me" and allows you to collect public reviews.
  • Trust Signals: Clearly display your business phone number, operating hours, and standard operating procedures (SOPs).

Day 2 • Module 1

Automated Attraction & Lead Magnets

Today is about generating traffic. Outbound cold-calling is exhausting. We want to build systems that bring owner-operators to you.

The Lead Magnet Strategy:

A Lead Magnet is a free, highly valuable digital resource you give away in exchange for a carrier's name, email, and phone number.

Example 1: The Lane Calculator

A simple Google Sheet that helps carriers calculate their exact cost-per-mile so they stop taking losing loads.

Example 2: Compliance Checklist

A PDF checklist of everything a new owner-operator needs to pass their new entrant safety audit.

Once they download your free guide, they enter your automated email sequence, positioning you as an expert until they are ready to hire a dispatcher.

Day 2 • Module 2

Social Media Authority

To promote your Lead Magnets, you need to establish authority on social channels where your targets hang out.

  • YouTube / TikTok: Create short, educational videos analyzing weekly freight rates, showing how to negotiate with brokers, or demonstrating how to use load boards. Owner-operators constantly search for this data.
  • Facebook Groups: Don't spam your services. Answer driver's questions. When someone complains about a bad broker, offer constructive advice. Your profile should link back to your website and Lead Magnet.
  • LinkedIn: Essential for connecting with Brokers and Direct Shippers. Post updates about your agency's reliability, on-time delivery percentages, and capacity availability.
Day 2 • Module 3

Securing Direct Shipper Freight

To retain the carriers you acquire, you must feed them excellent freight. Bypassing load boards to work directly with shippers is the ultimate goal.

The Shipper Pitch Workflow:

  1. Identify: Use Google Maps or local directories to find manufacturers, distributors, or agricultural hubs in your carrier's preferred outbound lanes.
  2. Research: Use LinkedIn to find the "Supply Chain Manager" or "Logistics Coordinator" at that facility.
  3. Outreach: Send a targeted email stating:
    "We have 5 dedicated flatbeds emptying out near your facility every Tuesday. Are you looking for guaranteed asset-based capacity to avoid spot market volatility?"
  4. Deliver: Be prepared to immediately send a professional Carrier Packet, MC Authority, and W-9.

Day 3 • Module 1

Consultative Selling (The Discovery Call)

When your inbound marketing works and a carrier calls you, the dynamic shifts. You are no longer begging for business; you are consulting to see if they are a fit for your agency.

The Discovery Framework:

  • Do not pitch immediately: Start by asking questions. "How long have you had your authority?" "What lanes do you hate running?"
  • Find the Pain: "What is your biggest headache right now—is it finding rates, or dealing with the broker paperwork at the end of the day?"
  • Present the Custom Solution: "Based on what you told me, you need a dispatcher who will strictly keep you out of the Northeast and handles 100% of the invoicing so you can get paid faster. That is exactly what our agency does."
Day 3 • Module 2

Professional Onboarding & Compliance

Once they say "yes," your onboarding process must be flawless. Sloppy paperwork scares away good carriers.

Required Legal Documents

  • Dispatch Agreement: Outlines your % fee and duties.
  • Limited POA: Legally authorizes you to sign rate cons.
  • W-9 & NOA: Required for broker setups.
  • Insurance Certs: Kept on file to send to brokers.

The Carrier Profile

A vital intake form where you define the boundaries of the partnership:

  • Hard minimum rate per mile.
  • Max gross weight limits.
  • Preferred regions / regions to avoid.
  • Home-time requirements.
