4.1 Brand Architecture: Branded House vs. House of Brands

Module 4 • Slide 1 of 6

Structuring multi-product enterprises requires deliberate brand architecture strategy.

  • Branded House: Single master brand covers all offerings (e.g., FedEx). High equity transfer.
  • House of Brands: Independent sub-brands operate autonomously (e.g., P&G). Isolated risk.
  • Hybrid Models: Endorsed or sub-branded structures bridging parent and product identities.
Evaluating branded house versus house of brands frameworks.

4.2 Sub-brands, Endorsed Brands, & Portfolio Logic

Module 4 • Slide 2 of 6

Balancing parent company backing with product-specific autonomy requires precise portfolio logic.

  • Endorsed Structures: Subsidiary maintains identity while leveraging parent trust (e.g., Courtyard by Marriott).
  • Portfolio Rationalization: Eliminating redundant sub-brands that dilute marketing resources.
  • Equity Spillover: Managing how crises in one sub-brand impact the broader portfolio.
Managing portfolio risk and equity spillover pathways.
