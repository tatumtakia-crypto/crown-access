1.1 Enterprise Architecture: Independent Dispatching vs. Brokerage

Module 1 • 45 Mins • Slide 1 of 5

Establishing a profitable logistics service business requires absolute clarity regarding industry roles and legal distinctions. Independent freight dispatching operates by representing motor carriers directly, serving as their remote back-office operations manager.

  • Regulatory Separation: Unlike freight brokers who interject themselves between shippers and carriers and are mandated by federal law to maintain a $75,000 surety bond, independent dispatchers act solely as agents for the carrier.
  • Financial Freedom & Overhead: Because you do not take financial custody of freight or handle broker bond underwriting, your initial startup overhead remains exceptionally low.
Understanding independent agent economics and regulatory structures.

1.2 Corporate Entity Formation: LLCs and EIN Acquisition

Module 1 • Slide 2 of 5

To operate with institutional credibility and protect your personal assets from corporate liabilities, you must establish a formal business entity before signing client contracts or opening commercial merchant accounts.

  • State Registration: Filing your Articles of Organization to establish your corporate LLC structure (such as within Jefferson County, Alabama, or your respective operating state).
  • Federal EIN: Securing your official Employer Identification Number directly from the IRS website.
  • Commercial Banking: Opening a dedicated business checking account to maintain clean corporate records.
Establishing LLC protection, EIN credentials, and commercial accounts.

1.3 Regulatory Compliance: MC, DOT, and State Licensing

Module 1 • Slide 3 of 5

Navigating motor carrier compliance ensures that your agency operates entirely above board and within federal transportation guidelines.

  • Motor Carrier Authorities: Understanding how your carrier clients obtain their active MC and USDOT numbers through the FMCSA.
  • Dispatcher Verification: Ensuring that every carrier you onboard maintains active authority, valid safety ratings, and clean compliance history.
  • Local Municipal Licensing: Checking county and city business license requirements where your agency headquarters is registered.
Verifying motor carrier safety ratings and regulatory compliance.

1.4 Revenue Architecture & Agency Financial Modeling

Module 1 • Slide 4 of 5

Structuring your agency's billing models and income projections determines your long-term scalability and cash flow stability.

  • Weekly Invoicing Cycles: Implementing automated weekly settlement statements tied directly to carrier load confirmations.

Modeling weekly gross revenue and percentage-based service fees.
