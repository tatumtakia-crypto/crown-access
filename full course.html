1.1 Course Introduction & The Dispatching Landscape

Module 1 • 45 Mins • Slide 1 of 4

Welcome to Crown Access. Independent freight dispatching is one of the most profitable, low-overhead service businesses you can launch.

  • Dispatcher vs. Broker: Dispatchers represent carriers and negotiate on their behalf; brokers act as intermediaries between shippers and carriers.
  • Independence: Operate your agency from anywhere with complete schedule autonomy.
  • Revenue Model: Earn consistent weekly percentages (typically 5% to 10%) per loaded truck.
Understanding the core mechanics of independent freight dispatching.

3.3 Carrier Documentation Vault

Module 3 • Slide 3 of 4

Brokers will request compliance paperwork every single time you book a load.

  • W-9 Form: Carrier's taxpayer identification and legal business address.
  • Certificate of Insurance (COI): Proof of active auto liability (typically $100k+) and cargo insurance ($100k+).
  • MC Authority Letter & NOA: Official FMCSA grant letter and Notice of Assignment (if using a factoring company).
Organizing carrier insurance, W-9s, and authority letters.

4.3 Equipment Types & Specifications

Module 4 • Slide 3 of 4

Match your carrier's exact equipment dimensions and weight limits to suitable loads.

  • Dry Van: Enclosed trailer for general palletized freight (53ft standard).
  • Reefer: Temperature-controlled refrigerated trailers for produce and pharmaceuticals.
  • Flatbed & Step Deck: Open-deck trailers for construction materials, machinery, and oversized cargo.
  • Box Trucks: Straight trucks for regional and dock-height deliveries.
Matching carrier equipment specifications to suitable freight categories.

Evaluating spot market volatility versus dedicated contract freight.

5.4 Handling Deadhead and Layover Negotiations

Module 5 • Slide 4 of 4

Ensure your carriers are compensated fairly when unexpected transit disruptions occur.

  • Detention Pay: Negotiating standard hourly rates (e.g., $50-$100/hr after 2 free hours) for loading/unloading delays.
  • Layover Compensation: Securing guaranteed daily pay if a shipper delays a load overnight.
  • Truck Ordered Not Used (TONU): Claiming fees when brokers cancel dispatched loads.
Securing detention pay, layover fees, and TONU compensation.
